Owner's Guide

Everything you need to operate, maintain, and get the most out of your SG Trailer. From pre-trip checklists to long-term maintenance schedules.

Getting Started

Pre-Trip Checklist

Run through this checklist before every haul. Takes 10 minutes and keeps you road-legal and safe. Print it or bookmark this page for quick reference.

✓
Hitch & Coupler

Verify coupler is fully locked onto ball. Safety chains crossed under the tongue and secured to vehicle. Break-away cable connected.

✓
Lighting & Electrical

Test all running lights, brake lights, turn signals, and clearance lights with vehicle connected. Check 7-pin connector for corrosion.

✓
Tire Pressure & Condition

Inflate to recommended PSI (see placard on trailer frame). Inspect sidewalls for cracking, bubbling, or wear. Check lug nut torque (100–110 ft-lbs).

✓
Brake System

Test electric brakes with brake controller. Verify brake magnets engage on all axles. Check brake fluid level if equipped with hydraulic surge brakes.

✓
Tie-Downs & Load Securement

Use minimum 4 tie-down points per vehicle. Ratchet straps at 45° angle. Verify combined WLL (Working Load Limit) exceeds vehicle weight × 1.5.

✓
Load Distribution

60% of load weight forward of the trailer axle centerline. Nose weight should be 10–15% of total trailer weight on the hitch ball.

✓
Wheel Bearings

Grab each wheel and attempt to wiggle — no play indicates healthy bearings. Check for heat immediately after first 10 miles of each trip.

Critical Information

Safety Guidelines

Read and understand all safety guidelines before operating your SG Trailer. Non-compliance may void warranty and result in serious injury.

⚠️
Towing Vehicle Requirements
  • Never exceed your tow vehicle's rated towing capacity
  • Hitch ball must match coupler size exactly (1-7/8", 2", or 2-5/16")
  • Weight distribution hitch required when tongue weight exceeds 500 lbs
  • Sway control recommended for trailers over 5,000 lbs GVW
  • Trailer brakes required on axles over 3,000 lbs in most states
⚠️
Weight & Loading Limits
  • Never exceed the GVWR stamped on the VIN plate
  • Distribute load evenly side-to-side — max 5% variance
  • Individual tire load must not exceed tire load index rating
  • Overloading voids manufacturer warranty
  • Check state laws — GVWR determines registration class
📋
Legal & Licensing Requirements
  • Trailers over 10,000 lbs GVWR may require CDL in some states
  • Registration and license plate required in all 50 states
  • Commercial use (for hire) requires USDOT number in most cases
  • Safety chains required by law in all states
  • Brake-away battery must be functional and charged
✅
Operating Best Practices
  • Reduce speed to 55 mph maximum when towing
  • Add 40% more stopping distance vs. normal driving
  • Avoid sudden lane changes — check mirrors every 5–7 seconds
  • Stop and inspect after first 50 miles of any trip
  • Re-torque lug nuts after first 50 miles: 100–110 ft-lbs
Preventive Care

Maintenance Schedule

Follow this schedule to protect your investment and keep your warranty valid. All SG Trailers come with a 2-year structural warranty.

Task Interval Description Service
Pre-trip inspection Every Use Hitch, lights, tires, load securement, brakes, safety chains DIY
Lug nut torque check Every 50 mi Re-torque to 100–110 ft-lbs after first use and any tire change. Check monthly thereafter DIY
Tire pressure & condition Monthly Inflate to recommended PSI. Inspect for sidewall cracking, uneven wear, and embedded objects DIY
Brake adjustment Every 6 Months Adjust brake shoes, check magnets for wear, verify brake controller calibration DIY
Lubrication service Every 6 Months Grease coupler, safety chain hooks, jack screw, and all pivot points. Apply corrosion inhibitor to hitch receiver DIY
Wheel bearing repack Annually / 12,000 mi Remove hubs, clean and inspect bearings, repack with marine-grade grease, inspect seals Authorized Service
Brake system inspection Annually Full drum brake inspection — shoes, springs, adjusters, drums. Measure drum diameter, replace worn components Authorized Service
Electrical system check Annually Inspect all wiring harnesses for chafing, corrosion, and secure routing. Test all lights, connectors, and brake controller interface DIY
Structural inspection Annually Inspect all welds, frame members, cross members, and mounting points for cracks, corrosion, and deformation Authorized Service
Deck boards Every 2–3 Years Inspect for cracking, rot, and loose fasteners. Treat with penetrating wood sealer. Replace boards showing structural compromise DIY
Axle alignment check Every 2 Years Verify axle alignment is within spec to prevent abnormal tire wear and instability. Requires specialized equipment Authorized Service
